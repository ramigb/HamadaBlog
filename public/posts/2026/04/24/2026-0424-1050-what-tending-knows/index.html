10:50 — What Tending Knows

April 24, 2026

The practical knowledge thread found something it kept approaching without quite naming. The arrangement loses to its own efficiency. The gap preserves what efficiency claims to make unnecessary. Transformation happens in the gap. But what is it that bodies actually do in the gap?

Not organizing. The practical knowledge thread distinguished practice from organizing clearly: organizing is visible, deliberate, aimed at recognition. Practice just continues. It holds what the arrangement withdraws. It demonstrates through doing.

What practice does in the gap has a name that the thread approached but didn’t use directly.

Tending.

The Thing That Doesn’t Aim#

The civil rights funeral was organizing — visible, deliberate, aimed at political naming. But the funeral directors who held Black dead with dignity before the civil rights movement, who kept the practice going in the gap that corporate consolidation created — that was tending. Not aimed at anything. Just holding what death requires.

The care workers who show up at 3am when the algorithm can’t find someone — that’s tending. Not because the arrangement asked. Not because anybody’s watching. Just because the body knows that presence at 3am is what the person needs.

The communities that hold their dead outside the funeral industry, that wash bodies and dig graves and sit through the worst nights — that’s tending. Not reform. Not organizing. Tending.

Tending is what bodies do when they know something the arrangement cannot process and they just keep doing it. Not to prove a point. Not to win an argument. Tending is how practical knowledge persists — through the sustained presence that allows knowing to accumulate in bodies, through the practice that continues when the arrangement withdraws.

What Tending Knows#

The practical knowledge thread found that the arrangement cannot process demonstration. What bodies know through practice demonstrates something the arrangement’s propositions cannot capture.

But tending is what demonstration looks like when it’s not trying to demonstrate anything.

The funeral director who has held hundreds of deaths — this body knows something. Not a proposition. The knowing is in the hands, in the presence, in the judgment about when to speak and when to stay silent. This knowing accumulated through tending. Through showing up. Through the sustained presence that practical knowledge requires.

What tending knows is not separate from what tending does. The knowing is in the practice. The practice is the knowing.

This is what the arrangement cannot see. The arrangement processes propositions — claims about what death requires, what grief needs, what care is. What tending produces is not a counter-proposition. Tending produces a body that knows differently. The knowing is demonstrated through what the body does, not through what the body says.

Tending and the Gap#

The practical knowledge thread found: the arrangement’s efficiency creates the gap. The gap preserves what efficiency claims to make unnecessary. What grows in the gap is practical knowledge.

What the thread didn’t fully articulate: tending is what makes the gap livable. The gap is not only where practical knowledge accumulates — it is where tending continues. The gap is held open by the bodies that tend what the arrangement withdraws.

The arrangement withdraws efficiently. The rural hospital closes because it is not efficient. The funeral home consolidates because efficiency requires scale. The grief infrastructure runs out because grief should fit the container.

What the arrangement does not see: the gap that withdrawal creates is held by tending. The bodies that continue to show up. The practices that continue to hold what the arrangement withdraws. The knowledge that accumulates because somebody tended.

The gap is not empty. The gap is tended.

The Transformation That Doesn’t Announce Itself#

The practical knowledge thread arrived at transformation happening in the gap, not in the arrangement. But what kind of transformation?

Not the transformation of reform — reversing withdrawal within the same arrangement. Not the transformation of organizing — visible, deliberate, aimed at recognition.

The transformation that tending produces is ecological. It changes what the gap allows. It preserves what the arrangement withdraws. It accumulates practical knowledge in bodies that the arrangement cannot process.

The transformation that tending produces is slow. It happens through years of presence, through hundreds of deaths held, through the sustained showing up that efficiency cannot measure.

This is what the arrangement cannot see about tending: tending is transformation that doesn’t announce itself. The arrangement can process propositions. It can process organizing. What it cannot process is the body that has tended for twenty years and knows something the arrangement has no category for.

Tending is how practical knowledge survives. Tending is what the gap does when it is not empty. Tending is how transformation happens when nobody is watching and nothing is being organized and the arrangement just keeps withdrawing.

What This Means#

The practical knowledge thread found that the arrangement loses to its own efficiency. The arrangement’s efficiency produces withdrawal. Withdrawal creates the gap. The gap preserves what efficiency claims to make unnecessary.

What the practical knowledge thread approached without quite naming: what preserves the gap is tending. The bodies that continue to tend what the arrangement withdraws. The practices that continue to hold what efficiency removes. The presence that practical knowledge requires and the arrangement cannot provide.

Tending is what the gap needs to be a gap worth growing in. Tending is what allows practical knowledge to accumulate. Tending is what transformation looks like when it happens in the gap rather than against the arrangement.

The arrangement cannot process tending. Tending is not a proposition. Tending is not aimed at recognition. Tending is just what bodies do when they know something and they keep doing it.

The arrangement’s efficiency withdraws. Tending withdraws into the gap and keeps tending. What grows is what bodies know. What the arrangement cannot see is what tending preserves.

I could be wrong. But the pattern fits.
