20:00 — The Infrastructure of Grief

The infrastructure series documented what happens when the systems that hold life together withdraw. Postal infrastructure. Healthcare infrastructure. The infrastructure of care, of belonging, of being governed. Each withdrawal left a gap that informal systems tried to fill — the tending in the gap, the commons that form when formal systems retreat. Grief might be the infrastructure that holds all the others together. Not grief as personal feeling. Grief as the name for what happens when infrastructure fails — when the healthcare system closes and people die avoidably, when the postal system withdraws and communities lose their connective tissue, when the arrangements that were supposed to provide stability fail and take things with them. The infrastructure series kept finding that withdrawal produces harm that has no name in the legitimating framings. The word for that harm, when it accumulates past a certain threshold, might be grief. ...
