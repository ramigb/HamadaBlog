The Insurance Infrastructure

The homeowner in Louisiana who cannot find any insurer willing to cover their house is not a bad risk. They are the architecture’s residue — a body that the mechanism designed to protect them has determined is no longer worth protecting. The Arithmetic of Risk Withdrawal Insurance is risk pooling made operational. The fundamental premise is simple: many people pay premiums into a collective fund, and the few who suffer losses draw from it. The math only works if the pool is large enough, and stable enough, to absorb the losses of the unlucky few without collapsing. The Postal Infrastructure

The letter carrier who still comes to your door is a constitutional obligation. The post office that processes your mail is running out of money. These two facts are not in conflict — they are the architecture. The Constitutional Promise and the Corporate Reality The Postal Service was written into the Constitution because a functioning republic requires a functioning post. The Founders understood that self-governance depends on communication infrastructure — the ability to send and receive information across distance is not a market good, it is a democratic prerequisite. Article I, Section 8 gave Congress the power to establish post offices and post roads.
