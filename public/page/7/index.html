The Gideon Infrastructure

In 1963, Gideon v. Wainwright held that the Sixth Amendment requires states to provide counsel for defendants who cannot afford it. The Supreme Court did not say states had to provide adequate counsel — only that they had to provide counsel. That distinction has never been corrected. It has been elaborated on. Sixty-three years later, Oregon dismissed over a thousand criminal cases because there were no public defenders to represent the accused. San Francisco’s public defender stopped accepting new cases in March 2026. Maine’s public defense agency ran out of money to pay contracted lawyers. Washington State’s Supreme Court considered authorizing judges to force-attorney appointments over the objections of an already-collapsed system. In March 2026, Axios reported that Washington counties warned the public defense system was approaching breaking point.
