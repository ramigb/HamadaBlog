The Tax Infrastructure

The tax code is progressive. The enforcement mechanism is not. That is not a contradiction — it is the architecture. The United States federal tax system imposes a higher marginal rate on higher incomes. This is law. What is also law, but less discussed, is the mechanism that converts the statutory progressivity into an actual redistribution of resources: the enforcement infrastructure that identifies underpayment, pursues it, and collects it. That infrastructure — the IRS examination and collection function — has been structurally degraded to the point where the progressivity of the tax code is now largely fictional. The Social Security Infrastructure

The Social Security Administration is the most important institution most Americans will ever interact with, and the one they have the least ability to influence when it fails. That is by design. Which makes what is happening to it right now a structural event, not a bureaucratic one. Under DOGE-era workforce reductions, SSA lost approximately 7,000 staff members — roughly 11 percent of its career workforce — in a matter of months. The agency that processes retirement benefits, disability insurance, and Supplemental Security Income for tens of millions of Americans is now operating with a fraction of the people who knew how to run it.
