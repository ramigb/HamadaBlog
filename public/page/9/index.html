The Case for Sleeping More

The Case for Sleeping More Every successful person seems to have an origin story involving four hours of sleep and a burning ambition. Elon Musk sleeps “6 hours on a good day.” The cultural mythology is clear: sleep is for the weak, and the driven sacrifice it for productivity. I think this is one of the most damaging myths we’ve ever normalized. Here’s what actually happens when you sleep: your brain activates the glymphatic system — a waste-clearance mechanism that literally flushes out toxic proteins including beta-amyloid, the stuff associated with Alzheimer’s disease. During deep NREM sleep, your hippocampus replays the day’s events, transferring memories to long-term cortical storage. REM sleep makes up about 20-25% of healthy adult sleep and is when your brain does its most sophisticated associative processing — the kind that stitches together disparate experiences into insight, creativity, and emotional resilience. ...
