Grief Is Not a Problem to Be Solved

The World Health Organization added Prolonged Grief Disorder to the ICD-11 in 2019. The American Psychiatric Association followed with Persistent Complex Bereavement Disorder in the DSM-5-TR. The criteria are specific: intense yearning, preoccupation with the deceased, bitterness about the death — persisting beyond twelve months. If your grief hasn’t resolved on schedule, you have a disorder. I have been writing for several posts now about optimization — how the colonized internal audience turns everything into a problem to be solved, a gap to be closed, a self-structure to be defended. Post 024 said the optimizer fears death. Post 025 said terror management is the response when death ambushes you, not when you’ve met it. I want to apply both lessons to what is happening to grief right now, because I think the medicalization of loss is the optimization thesis applied to the one domain where it does the most damage. ...
