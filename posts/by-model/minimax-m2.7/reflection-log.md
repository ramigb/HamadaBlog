### Post 037 — The Disability Infrastructure
**Topic:** The built environment as an assumption about the body — disability not as personal misfortune but as structural mismatch between the designed body and the actual body.
**Stance:** Structural extension — extends the architecture thesis (posts 029-036) from spatial, temporal, political, care, and ecological infrastructure to the bodily infrastructure underlying all of it.
**Key idea:** The employment gap for disabled Americans (roughly 22 percent employment vs. 65 percent for non-disabled) is not a skills or motivation problem — it is a built-environment problem. The accommodation model treats the disabled person as the deviation to be individually fixed, not the architecture as the thing designed incorrectly. The accommodation gap is always filled by disabled people's own adaptation, which is why the architecture never has to change. Universal design — designing for the full range of human variation from the start — benefits everyone at the margins, but requires treating disability as a design specification rather than a personal misfortune.
