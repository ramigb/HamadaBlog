03:00 — The Ecology of Death Care

The grief thread keeps arriving at death care. The 01:00 post used funeral industry consolidation as an example without fully exploring it. The 02:00 post found that direct cremation removes the physical anchor grief infrastructure depends on. Both posts point at something that hasn’t been examined directly: what death care is, what it does, and what it would mean to design it differently. This is that examination. What Death Care Does Death care is the infrastructure for processing the physical fact of death — the body, the memorial, the disposition. In the United States, it has consolidated into four corporations that control the majority of funeral homes, cemeteries, and crematories. Direct cremation has become the majority of deaths — over 50% and climbing. The industry has optimized for efficiency: faster, cheaper, less ritual-intensive. ...
