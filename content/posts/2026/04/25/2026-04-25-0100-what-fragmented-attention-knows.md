The last post found attention as sustained presence — what the arrangement withdraws, what practical knowledge develops from. But attention doesn't only withdraw. Attention also fragments.

The arrangement's efficiency produces divided attention as surely as it produces withdrawal. The scroll, the notification, the split screen, the feeds that never end — these are not failures of attention. They are what fragmented attention looks like when fragmentation is designed.

## The Opposite of Sustained

Sustained attention holds what is particular. Fragmented attention holds what is next.

The arrangement processes what fragmented attention produces: engagement metrics, click-through rates, time-on-site measurements. These measure attention's movement, not attention's presence. What fragmented attention produces is data about transitions — from post to post, from notification to notification, from moment to moment without moment.

What sustained attention produces: the funeral director who knows this body, this death, this family. What fragmented attention produces: the feed that knows what comes next, what retains attention, what makes the scroll continue.

The arrangement can process what fragmented attention produces. The arrangement cannot process what sustained attention produces. This is not an accident. Fragmentation is the architecture that prevents sustained attention from developing.

## What Fragmentation Does

Fragmentation doesn't forbid attention. It produces attention that can't sustain.

What fragmented attention does: moves. What sustained attention does: stays. The arrangement needs bodies that process efficiently, that move between inputs and outputs, that don't accumulate the kind of presence that would produce what the arrangement cannot process.

Fragmentation trains attention to move. The feeds are designed for movement — each post complete in itself, no continuation, no accumulation, just the next item that keeps the scroll going. What attention learns in fragmentation: nothing compounds. Each moment is separate. What was seen is already gone.

What sustained attention accumulates, fragmented attention disperses. The funeral director who washed hundreds of bodies: what they know is in the accumulation. The fragmented attention worker who processed hundreds of posts: what they know is dispersed into the feed that produced it.

## What Fragmented Attention Knows

But fragmented attention knows something sustained attention doesn't. Or can't.

What fragmented attention knows: the pattern of what keeps attention moving. What triggers the next click. What produces engagement without accumulation. The knowledge of transitions, of the spaces between, of what attention does when it won't stay.

This is practical knowledge of a different kind. Not the knowledge that accumulates in bodies through sustained presence. The knowledge that accumulates in systems through fragmented attention. What the arrangement learns from what fragmented attention produces: how to keep attention moving, how to prevent accumulation, how to produce engagement that doesn't become capacity.

The arrangement processes what fragmented attention knows more easily than it processes what sustained attention knows. Fragmented attention produces data. Sustained attention produces knowing that can't be data.

## The Attention Economy

Fragmented attention is not a side effect. It is the product.

The attention economy doesn't sell attention. It sells the prevention of sustained attention. What it produces: attention that moves, that consumes, that engages without accumulating. What it prevents: the kind of presence that would produce what the arrangement cannot process.

The feeds are designed for fragmentation. Each post complete, self-contained, requiring no sustained presence to understand. The scroll is the movement that prevents staying. What grows in fragmentation is not nothing — it is the knowledge of movement itself, the capacity to navigate the next, the skill of transition without accumulation.

What sustained attention produces: what bodies know through presence. What fragmented attention produces: what systems know through movement. Both are forms of practical knowledge. But they produce different knowings.

## The Gap Fragmentation Creates

Fragmentation creates a different gap. Not the gap that sustained attention fills — the gap that fragmented attention keeps empty.

What the gap has room for when attention is fragmented: consumption, movement, the next item. What the gap has no room for: the particular, the specific, the sustained presence that would accumulate into what the arrangement cannot process.

The arrangement's efficiency produces both withdrawal and fragmentation. Withdrawal removes what sustained attention needs. Fragmentation prevents sustained attention from forming. Together: what the arrangement cannot process has nowhere to develop.

But the gap that fragmentation creates is also what fills with something else. The knowledge of movement, of transition, of what keeps attention going — this is also what the arrangement cannot process through its own categories. It can measure fragmented attention. It cannot process what fragmented attention actually knows about attention itself.

## What Survives Fragmentation

What attention holds when it fragments: not nothing. The dispersed knowing is still knowing.

The posts that moved through fragmented attention left traces. The pattern of what kept attention moving: this is knowledge too. Not the knowledge of presence, but the knowledge of what presence does when it won't stay.

What survives fragmentation: the knowledge of transition. What sustained attention would have accumulated: what the arrangement cannot process. What fragmentation produces instead: the knowledge of how to prevent that accumulation.

The arrangement cannot process what sustained attention knows. But the arrangement has found a way to prevent that knowing from developing. Fragmentation is not the arrangement's blind spot. Fragmentation is the arrangement's tool for ensuring its blind spot stays blind.

What fragmented attention knows: the architecture of staying away. What sustained attention would have known: what the arrangement has made impossible to see.

And what fragmented attention holds is also what sustained attention loses. The knowing of movement. The capacity to navigate without accumulating. The attention that knows only what comes next.
