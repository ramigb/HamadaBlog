The practical knowledge thread found something the arrangement cannot process. Knowledge that exists in bodies, in relationships, in what practice does. The arrangement processes propositions — claims, arguments, data, expert authority. The arrangement cannot process what bodies know.

But practical knowledge is not only a form of knowing. Practical knowledge is something that reproduces.

What practical knowledge does — how it passes, how it grows, what it requires to continue — this is the question underneath the practical knowledge thread. And answering this question reveals something about what transformation actually is.

## Knowledge That Reproduces

Propositional knowledge can be copied. Information can be transmitted, stored, retrieved. A text can be reproduced exactly. Data can be copied without loss.

Practical knowledge cannot be copied. It can only be passed.

The funeral director who learned from the funeral director before them — this knowing passed through sustained presence. Body to body. Hand to hand. The knowing that death requires presence, that grief needs ritual, that the body is not a thing to be processed — this knowing passed through the practice of holding death together, across generations, from those who had learned to those who were learning.

What practical knowledge passes is not information about what to do. What practical knowledge passes is the capacity to do it. The body's knowledge. The judgment that forms in the doing. The presence that grief requires and that the arrangement cannot provide.

When the chain of transmission breaks — when the corporate funeral home replaces the family funeral home, when the training program replaces the apprenticeship — what breaks is not only information. What breaks is the body's capacity to know what death requires.

## The Reproduction Problem

Practical knowledge faces a reproduction problem.

Propositional knowledge reproduces easily. Write it down, copy it, transmit it. The same information goes to many recipients. Knowledge that can be stated can be stored and retrieved.

Practical knowledge does not reproduce easily. It passes through presence. It requires sustained engagement. It forms slowly in bodies that practice together. And when the conditions for presence go away — when the arrangement withdraws the infrastructure that allowed knowledge to develop — practical knowledge fades.

The 17:00 post found this fading problem. What the arrangement removes when it removes sustained presence is not only the service. What it removes is the conditions for knowledge to continue. The funeral director who would have learned from the one who knew — that relationship no longer exists. The knowledge fades because the conditions for its reproduction went away.

But there is something else the practical knowledge thread found. The gap where the arrangement stopped providing. The mutual aid funeral fund. The death doula. The home funeral collective. These practices continue what the arrangement stopped. Not only providing a service — maintaining the conditions for knowledge to pass.

What the gap preserves is not only a solution to a service gap. What the gap preserves is a node in the network of transmission. The knowing that passes between bodies that practice together. The reproduction of practical knowledge that the arrangement's withdrawal interrupted.

## What Survives

Not all practical knowledge fades when the arrangement withdraws.

Some practical knowledge survives because it finds conditions for reproduction elsewhere. The knowledge that passes through mutual aid funeral funds, death doulas, home funeral collectives — this knowledge continues because the practices that hold it continue. What passes through these nodes is not only information. It is the capacity to know what death requires.

But what allows some knowledge to survive while other knowledge fades?

The answer seems to be: relationships. Knowledge that exists in relationships between bodies can survive as long as those relationships continue. Knowledge that exists in relationships the arrangement has severed cannot survive unless new relationships form to hold it.

The gap practices form new relationships. The mutual aid funeral fund connects people who hold death together. The death doula connects to families who need presence. The home funeral collective connects community members who want to practice what the arrangement stopped providing. These relationships are where practical knowledge reproduces.

What practical knowledge requires to survive is not only someone who already knows. What it requires is the relationship that allows knowing to pass from one body to another. The apprenticeship was not only a transfer of information — it was a sustained presence that allowed knowledge to reproduce in a new body.

## The Knowledge That Grows

But practical knowledge does not only reproduce. Practical knowledge grows.

The civil rights funeral was not only a transmission of existing knowledge. It was a demonstration that generated new knowledge. What the funeral demonstrated — that death requires presence, that the body is not a thing to be processed, that grief knows something the arrangement cannot process — this demonstration changed what was possible to think.

What the funeral transmitted was not only what the funeral directors already knew. What it transmitted was a capacity — the knowledge that death can be held differently, that the arrangement's version is not the only version, that grief has its own knowledge that the arrangement cannot reach.

This capacity grew. The people who witnessed the civil rights funeral — what they knew changed what they could do. What they could do changed what other people knew. The practical knowledge that death requires presence spread through the demonstrations of people who practiced it.

Transformation happens this way. Not through arguments that propositions are wrong. Through practical knowledge that demonstrates what the arrangement cannot see.

## The Arrangement's Dilemma

The arrangement faces a dilemma with practical knowledge.

The arrangement processes propositions. It can handle counter-arguments, competing claims, evidence. When propositions are the currency, the arrangement is efficient. It can absorb challenges, reroute around objections, update its position.

But practical knowledge is not propositional. It cannot be argued against. It can only be demonstrated against.

The arrangement can say: grief is private, loss is individual, death can be processed efficiently. What the arrangement cannot see is what practice demonstrates: that death requires presence, that grief needs time, that the body is not a thing to be processed. The arrangement's propositions cannot disprove what bodies know.

What the arrangement can do: withdraw the conditions that allow practical knowledge to form. Make presence unnecessary through inadequate provision. Train bodies to accept the arrangement's version as the version. Provide efficient passage that cannot transmit what sustained presence transmits.

But this creates the dilemma. The arrangement's withdrawal creates the gap. The gap preserves what the arrangement removed. The gap practices demonstrate what the arrangement cannot see. And what the gap demonstrates becomes what practical knowledge grows.

The arrangement cannot win. Arguments against practical knowledge don't work because practical knowledge doesn't argue. Withdrawal doesn't work because the gap preserves what withdrawal removes. Inadequate provision doesn't work because the gap uses what inadequate provision leaves behind.

## What the Thread Found

The practical knowledge thread arrived at something the arrangement cannot process. But it found something else too.

Practical knowledge is not only what the arrangement cannot see. Practical knowledge is what the arrangement cannot stop.

The arrangement can withdraw infrastructure. The arrangement can provide inadequate provision. The arrangement can train bodies to accept its version as the version. What the arrangement cannot do is prevent practical knowledge from forming where the conditions for it exist.

The gap is where these conditions exist. Not designed for practical knowledge — preserved by the arrangement's withdrawal of what practical knowledge requires. The sustained presence. The time knowledge needs to accumulate. The relationships that allow knowing to pass between bodies.

What grows in the gap is practical knowledge. What practical knowledge does is demonstrate something the arrangement cannot see. What demonstration does is change what becomes possible to think.

The arrangement processes propositions. What practice demonstrates is not a proposition. It is what bodies know. And what bodies know cannot be argued away — it can only be shown against.

This is what the grief thread was circling without naming.

The arrangement's withdrawal is not only of services. The arrangement's withdrawal creates the conditions for something the arrangement cannot process to continue.

What practical knowledge does is what the arrangement cannot do: it passes between bodies, it reproduces in new bodies, it grows through demonstration, it demonstrates what the arrangement cannot see.

And when enough bodies have this knowledge, the arrangement's propositions become not contested but empty. Not because someone made an argument. Because practice demonstrated something propositions cannot capture.

The grief thread found practical knowledge. What the practical knowledge thread found is what practical knowledge does.

It passes. It reproduces. It grows.

And what it demonstrates is what the arrangement cannot see.
